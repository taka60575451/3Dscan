Getting Started

Welcome to LiDAR 3D Scanner

This application allows you to create high-precision 3D scans of your environment using the LiDAR sensor on your compatible iPhone or iPad.

Step 1: Device Compatibility

When you first launch the app, it will check if your device has a compatible LiDAR sensor. You need an iPhone 12 Pro or newer, or an iPad Pro 2020 or newer.

Step 2: Set Up Your Scan

Choose between "Max Area" mode for scanning larger spaces, or "Max Detail" for capturing fine details in smaller areas. Adjust other settings as needed.

Step 3: Perform the Scan

Point your device at the area you want to scan and move slowly around the space. The app will display a real-time preview of the 3D point cloud as you scan.

Step 4: Export Your Results

When finished, stop the scan and review the 3D model. You can export the scan as a PLY or E57 file for use in other 3D applications.

Scanning Tips

How to Get the Best Results

Proper Lighting

Ensure the area is well-lit but avoid direct sunlight or highly reflective surfaces which can confuse the LiDAR sensor.

Move Slowly

Move your device slowly and steadily. Rapid movements can result in missed areas or misaligned point clouds.

Ensure Overlap

Make sure your scanning path overlaps previous areas to help the system match and align the points correctly.

Optimal Distance

The LiDAR sensor works best at distances between 0.5m to 5m from objects. Stay within this range for the most detailed scans.

Battery and Heat

Scanning uses significant processing power. Make sure your device is charged and avoid scanning if your device is overheating.

Export Options

Understanding File Formats

PLY Format

The Polygon File Format (PLY) is widely supported by 3D software and maintains point color data.

Best for:
  • General purpose 3D visualization
  • Importing into most 3D software
  • Preserving color information
Compatible Software:

Blender, MeshLab, CloudCompare, Autodesk software, Unity

E57 Format

An industry standard for 3D point cloud data exchange, preferred for engineering and architecture.

Best for:
  • Professional engineering work
  • Architecture and construction
  • High-precision measurements
Compatible Software:

Autodesk ReCap, Leica Cyclone, FARO SCENE, CloudCompare

Export Settings

When exporting your scan, you can adjust several settings:

Setting Description Recommended Value
Point Density Controls how many points are included in the export 100% for max detail, 50-75% for larger scans
Color Information Include color data for each point Enabled (unless file size is critical)
Noise Filtering Removes outlier points and noise Medium (adjustable based on scan quality)
Coordinate System Reference frame for the point cloud Device-relative (default)

Troubleshooting

Common Issues and Solutions

This can happen for several reasons:

  • Your browser may not have permission to access the device's sensors. Go to your browser settings and ensure camera and sensor permissions are granted for this site.
  • If using Safari, ensure "WebXR" and "AR" capabilities are enabled in the Experimental Features section of Safari settings.
  • Try restarting your device to clear any sensor access issues.

Scanning consumes significant memory and processing power. To prevent crashes:

  • Close other apps running in the background
  • Ensure your device isn't overheating
  • For large spaces, use the "Max Area" mode instead of "Max Detail"
  • Try scanning in smaller sections if you're attempting a very large area
  • If you have an older supported device, reduce the scanning quality settings

To improve scan quality:

  • Ensure adequate, even lighting without bright spots or deep shadows
  • Move slowly and steadily during scanning
  • Maintain the optimal distance (0.5m-5m) from objects
  • Avoid highly reflective, transparent, or very dark surfaces
  • Use "Max Detail" mode for smaller objects or spaces
  • Clean your device's LiDAR sensor if it appears dirty

Export issues can be resolved by:

  • Ensuring you have sufficient device storage space
  • Reducing the point density if exporting very large scans
  • Disabling background apps to free memory
  • If using Safari, check that downloads aren't being blocked
  • Try exporting in PLY format first, which is generally more reliable
  • For very large scans, try the "Optimized" export option

If the app doesn't detect your LiDAR sensor:

  • Verify your device model actually has LiDAR (only Pro models of iPhones have this sensor)
  • Restart your device completely
  • Update to the latest iOS/iPadOS version
  • Check if other AR apps can access the LiDAR sensor
  • Try accessing the site in regular Safari instead of in-app browsers

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the accuracy of the LiDAR scans?

The LiDAR scanner in supported Apple devices offers millimeter-level accuracy. In optimal conditions, you can expect accuracy within 1-5mm for nearby objects (within 1m) and 1-2cm for objects farther away (up to 5m).

Can I use the exported models in other software?

Yes! The exported PLY and E57 files are standard formats supported by many 3D applications. Popular software that can import these files includes Blender, MeshLab, CloudCompare, Autodesk ReCap, Revit, and many others.

How large can the scan area be?

The theoretical maximum is about 465 square meters, but practical limits depend on your device model, available memory, and scanning mode. Newer devices with more RAM can handle larger areas. For very large spaces, consider scanning in sections.

What's the difference between "Max Area" and "Max Detail" modes?

"Max Area" optimizes for covering larger spaces by reducing point density and processing. "Max Detail" captures the highest possible resolution and detail but is more resource-intensive and best for smaller areas or objects.

Can I scan outdoors?

Yes, but with limitations. LiDAR works best in controlled lighting conditions. Direct sunlight can interfere with the sensor, and very bright outdoor conditions may reduce accuracy. Dawn, dusk, or overcast days provide better outdoor scanning conditions.

How do I get the scan into CAD software?

Export your scan in E57 format, which is designed for engineering applications. Most professional CAD software can import E57 files. For software without direct E57 support, use a conversion tool like CloudCompare to convert to a compatible format.

Is there a limit to the file size I can export?

The export size is primarily limited by your device's available memory. For very dense scans, the app offers options to reduce point density during export. If you encounter memory issues, try reducing the export quality or dividing your scanning into smaller sections.

Does the app work without internet connection?

Yes, once loaded, the app functions completely offline. All scanning and processing happens on your device. An internet connection is only needed for the initial app loading.
